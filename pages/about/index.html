About

My name is Bailee Egan, and I am currently an M.S. student currently working under Dr. Etsuko Moriyama at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, studying the western corn rootworm and carbon-dioxide receptors. My main interests are bioinformatics, and my goals during my masters are to learn more about the tools of bioinformatics as well as the field itself. In my undergrad, I worked in the lab of Dr. Hope Hollocher at the University of Notre Dame, studying variation in the oral and gut microbiomes of long-tailed macaques. In my work, I mainly use Bash, Python, and R for processing data, but I'm familiar with Javascript, HTML, and CSS for developing small applets and tools.

I also enjoy drawing and painting. At Notre Dame, I worked as a comic artist for the campus newspaper, making hit-and-miss comics.
