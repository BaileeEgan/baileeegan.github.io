About

My name is Bailee Egan, and I am a bioinformatician by training with additional interests in data science and software development.

I received my M.S. in biology in 2022 at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. Under Dr. Etsuko Moriyama, I studied corn rootworm chemoreceptors, learning many of the tools and techniques of bioinformatics. In my undergrad, I worked in the lab of Dr. Hope Hollocher at the University of Notre Dame, studying variation in the oral and gut microbiomes of long-tailed macaques.

I have a passion for coding. In my work, I mainly use Bash, Python, and R for processing data. It is also no surprise that data visualization is one of my favorite parts of my work. In my personal projects, I've also learned Javascript, HTML, and CSS for developing small applets and tools, such as a rubric application for grading and a multiple sequence alignment viewer (SMMSA). I have also used the Godot Game Engine for experimenting with small games, shaders, and apps. Using Godot and Rust, I built a 3D terrain editing application.
