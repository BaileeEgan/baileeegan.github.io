About

My name is Bailee Egan, and I am currently an M.S. student currently working under Dr. Etsuko Moriyama at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, studying the western corn rootworm and carbon-dioxide receptors. My main interests are bioinformatics, and my goals during my masters are to learn more about the tools of bioinformatics as well as the field itself. In my undergrad, I worked in the lab of Dr. Hope Hollocher at the University of Notre Dame, studying variation in the oral and gut microbiomes of long-tailed macaques.

I have a passion for coding. In my work, I mainly use Bash, Python, and R for processing data. It is also no surprise that data visualization is one of my favorite parts of research. In my personal projects, I've also learned Javascript, HTML, and CSS for developing small applets and tools, such as a rubric application for grading and a multiple sequence alignment viewer (SMMSA).
